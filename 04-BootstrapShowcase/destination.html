Machu Picchu, Peru

Machu Picchu is an ancient Inca city located in the Cusco Region of Peru. It is one of the most popular tourist destinations in South America, and is known for its stunning mountaintop setting and well-preserved ruins.

  • Province/State: Cusco
  • Country: Peru
  • Population: 447 (2017)
  • Latitude & longitude: -13.1633° S, 72.5455° W

Great Barrier Reef, Australia

The Great Barrier Reef is the world's largest coral reef system composed of over 2,900 individual reefs and 900 islands stretching for over 2,300 kilometres over an area of approximately 344,400 square kilometres. The reef is located in the Coral Sea, off the coast of Queensland, Australia. The reef can be seen from outer space and is the world's biggest single structure made by living organisms. This reef structure is composed of and built by billions of tiny organisms, known as coral polyps. It supports a wide diversity of life and was selected as a World Heritage Site in 1981. CNN labelled it one of the seven natural wonders of the world.

  • Province/State: Queensland
  • Country: Australia
  • Population: 0
  • Latitude & longitude: -18.3000° S, 147.5000° E

Northern Lights, Iceland

The Northern Lights, also known as the Aurora Borealis, are a natural light display in the sky, predominantly seen in the high-latitude regions (around the Arctic and Antarctic). The effect is caused by the collision of charged particles from the sun with atoms in the Earth's atmosphere. The Northern Lights are typically green, but can also appear red, blue, or purple.

  • Province/State: All over Iceland
  • Country: Iceland
  • Population: 366,425 (2023)
  • Latitude & longitude: 64.9631° N, -19.0208° W

Serengeti National Park, Tanzania
